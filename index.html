Sea Otter Facts

Sea otters are marine mammals native to the coasts of the northern and eastern North Pacific Ocean. They are the smallest marine mammal species in North America but are known for their significant role in maintaining the health of the kelp forest ecosystems. Want to learn more fun facts? Ways to Help

Sea otters face various threats, including oil spills, habitat degradation, pollution, and predation by sharks and killer whales. Conservation efforts, such as habitat protection, pollution control, and anti-poaching measures, are crucial for the survival of sea otter populations. Sea otters face many threats.
